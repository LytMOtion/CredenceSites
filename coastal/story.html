Story · the land

The making of the course.

Ocean Bluff began with the coastline itself—a modern course shaped by wind, dunes, firm ground, native landscape, and the changing character of the Pacific.

Fictional demonstration property with sample content and reference imagery.

The intent was never to impose a course on the land, but to draw one out of it — to let firm coastal ground, native grasses, and the marine layer set the terms, and to see what kind of round the coastline wanted to give.

Where the land meets the water.

Conceptual routing of the inland front nine and coastal stretch. The front nine works the rolling ground inland; the back nine falls to the bluff, where holes 10–14 run the water's edge.

The routing

Drawn to the water's edge.

Holes turn where the ground turns; the ocean arrives when the land runs out. The coastline sets the rhythm of the round—nothing forced against its grain.

Firm turf, native grasses, and a low marine layer give the course its character. The same edge that shapes the dunes shapes the back nine — the round takes on the mood of the coast.

That is the whole idea, kept simple: ground shaped by the coast, and a round that takes its rhythm from the water.
